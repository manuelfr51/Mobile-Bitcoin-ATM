What Does CoinSafe Do?

We store bitcoin offline in physical bank vaults in three US states to ensure they are never lost or stolen. For the technically minded, it's more accurate to say that we store highly redundant copies of bitcoin private keys on encrypted USB drives that are kept in extremely secure facilities.

How Does CoinSafe Work?

You begin by creating an account. After registration, you are given your own personal Bitcoin address. This address is entirely on the blockchain and will only be shared with you. It works like any other Bitcoin address - you are free to send any amount to it at anytime, and it will safely remain in deep cold storage for as long as you'd like. Once you are ready to make a withdrawal, you submit a withdrawal request through our site and tell us where you would like the bitcoin sent. After we have verified that the request really came from you, two members of our transaction signing committee will travel to one of our bank locations to sign the transaction with your private keys. The withdrawal transaction is cryptographically signed within the bank vault so that your private key never leaves the premises. Then we take the signed transaction (which cannot be edited) back to the office and broadcast it to the bitcoin network to return your funds to you.

What Is Cold Storage?

Most bitcoin wallets are considered "hot", meaning that funds can be spent immediately. This is great when you want to use them (i.e. to pay for lunch), but also a great target for thieves.

Cold storage refers to keeping your bitcoin offline so that it isn't accessible to hackers and malware. Cold storage is a way to give up some convenience (being able to spend all of your money instantly) for added security (not having someone else able to do that). You can read more about cold storage on the official bitcoin wiki here.

How Do You Ensure My Bitcoin Is Never Lost?

By taking every extreme precaution.

We use bank vaults with encrypted USB drives in three non-neighboring US states. Each bank vault has 4 redundant USB drives made by 4 different drive manufacturers with an encrypted copy of all your private keys. We store the drives on high shelves in case of flooding, in plastic bags in case the sprinkler system goes off, and inside of Faraday Bags in case of an EMP. We cycle in new USB drives periodically as well.

Even if two of our bank vaults were simultaneously vaporized with our entire team inside them, none of our users would lose any bitcoin.

How Do You Ensure My Bitcoin Is Never Stolen?

With CoinSafe, no bitcoin private keys ever touch an internet connected machine. Even if an attacker were able to compromise our server, there are never any bitcoin there to steal.

In order to prevent someone from impersonating you to withdraw your bitcoin, every single withdrawal requires three factor authentication at a minimum:

  • Login with your username and password: your username is not your email, so you're covered case you were using that password on another site that got hacked
  • Receive a code via SMS: an attacker would have to compromise your phone as well
  • Click a link in your email: an attacker would have to compromise your email as well

In addition, we highly encourage each of our users to add a vault guardian. This is somebody you trust (a friend, family member, lawyer, etc) who must also sign-off on your withdrawals. We're always looking into ways to add even more security to accounts without sacrificing too much on convenience, please contact us if you have any ideas.

I'm A Security Nerd - How Do You Protect Your Servers?

None of our private keys ever touch the internet (or leave our bank vaults) so there is no way for a hacker to steal your bitcoin. That being said, theoretically a hacker could gain access to our server and attempt to trick us into processing a fraudulent withdrawal request. To protect against this, we have designed a system that we believe is unlike any other (extensive blog post forthcoming!).

When a new user registers, all of their data is immediately backed up on a completely separate cluster of severs. These machines are behind a very strict firewall that only accept incoming connections via 2FA ssh and never from the web server. Their job is to continuously "poll" the web server to check for any data discrepancies (e.g. a verification phone number being changed) as well as any unexpected changes in the code base. If anything fishy was ever detected, we would immediately be notified and would halt all trips to the bank vault until our team got to the bottom of it.

Above all, we believe we are more secure than anyone else because of the human layer we add. If anything ever feels off, we will not hesitate to pick up the phone and call you directly. Our job is to be overly cautious so you don't have to be!

How Long Does It Take to Get My Funds Back?

We're really good for security, but not great for speed. If you're looking to daytrade, CoinSafe is not a good choice for you.

Your bitcoin private keys are kept on encrypted USB drives in US bank vaults. Accessing them requires two members of our transaction signing committee to simultaneously enter their decryption keys into an offline machine inside the bank vault. This can take up to 2 business days, though we're usually faster.

The best practice in bitcoin security is to keep most of your funds offline in cold storage and then keep only a small ammount in warm/hot wallets and transfer between the two periodically. Think of your hot wallet like you checking account and your cold storage like your savings account.

How Long Does It Take to Deposit Funds?

You can deposit funds instantly at anytime. Once your account is setup, you don't need to ever contact us before a deposit, just make them whenever you like.

The way the bitcoin protocol works, a machine doesn't need to be online to accept transactions (that happens automatically by miners and is recorded in the blockchain). That means you can deposit anytime and your bitcoin private keys stay safely offline in our bank vaults. The only time we need to access the private keys is when we send your bitcoin back to you.

How Much Does CoinSafe Cost?

The first 10 bitcoins you store are 100% free! Above that, we charge 1% of the deposit amount per year in bitcoin. This amount is annualized based on fractions of a year (e.g. 0.5% for 6 months). We don't collect our fee until you withdraw.

To clarify, if you store 15 bitcoin for 6 months, we would only charge you for storing 5 bitcoins (the first 10 are always free). Since 6 months is half of a year, we'd charge 0.5% for those 5 bitcoins, or 0.025 BTC in total. So, we'd send you back 14.975 BTC of your 15 BTC, for an average fee of ~0.167%.

Our prices are strictly in Bitcoin and we don't accept government currencies. We don't adjust our pricing if the value of a bitcoin (in government currencies) goes up or down.

Why Doesn't CoinSafe Offer Insurance?

We're in talks with major underwriters and trying to be the first ones to offer a capitalized and publicly verifiable insurance plan, but nobody has been able to do this yet :(

Some bitcoin companies have engaged in unethical insurance practices like:

  • Claiming they had insurance that they didn't
  • Buying a data breach plan that covers against damages arising from hackers stealing personally identifiable information (PII) like social security numbers and trying to use this for bitcoin. Even worse, the bitcoin is supposed to be kept offline, so it really doesn't apply.
  • Self-insuring through their own subsidiary/captive that doesn't have capital to pay losses, while not disclosing any of this info

Do your homework very careful to be sure that any "insurance" you're paying for is real. Real insurance plans are always publicly verifiable and have the capital to cover loss events if they occur. The underwriter should have enough capital so that in the event of a claim they don't have to decide between paying it and going out of business, or fighting it and staying alive. Feel free to contact us if you're considering using a service that claims to offer insurance but doesn't doesn't meet these basic standards and we'd be happy to evaluate it for you.

Is it Safe to Store Bitcoin on My Desktop Computer?

Here are the main problems associated with storing bitcoin on your personal computer:

  • Your computer is connected to the internet which is really dangerous. We cannot stress this enough: you should only store an amount of bitcoin you're comfortable losing on internet connected machines.
  • Your computer may be infected with malware. Over 40% of all personal computers are infected with malware and bitcoin wallets are a prime target. This happens far too often and is very difficult to detect until it is too late.
  • Everything must be backed up in case of hardware failure. Furthermore, these backups must be properly encrypted and stored in geographically diverse locations to protect against theft, fire, and acts of nature.
  • There is no room for mistakes. Bitcoin is quite complex, and stories of simple oversights causing catastrophic results are unfortunately too common. The Bitcoin protocol is incredibly secure, but only when everything is done properly.

It's not a bad idea to keep a small amount of funds on a desktop computer, but probably no more than you would hold in your physical wallet unless you really know what you're doing.

How is CoinSafe Different from a Hot Wallet Like blockchain.info?

Blockchain.info offers a solid hot wallet that we recommend for storing small amounts of funds. The main difference is that with blockchain.info is that the information necessary to spend your bitcoin exists on an internet-connected machines. You control a password to unlock those funds, so theoretically your bitcoin is secure. There are many instances in which a bug, malicious employee, or socially engineered attack could lead someone to steal your bitcoin from that machine.

To protect your funds, there is no way to reset your password if you forget it or transfer your bitcoin to your estate if you die.

Also, as a foreign company they're not bound by US laws.

How is CoinSafe Different from an Off-Blockchain Wallets like Coinbase and Mt. Gox?

Since these companies don't open-source their code and processes, we can only infer how their system works based on the limited info they do share. Their basic model is that they hold a lot of bitcoin in many pooled addresses (most of which are offline) and internally they keep track of which users are owed which bitcoin. They also engage in a broker-dealer business of buying and selling bitcoin, which they probably make a profit on. Like all risk-taking enterprises it has the potential to fail catastrophically.

While all transactions in the blockchain are public, Coinbase and Mt. Gox's pooling of funds makes it nearly impossible to measure or audit if they are solvent. A hacker (or disgruntled employee) could steal a large percentage of their bitcoin and users wouldn't have any way to know. It's possible that they've lost private keys to wallets.

We have no idea what losses these off-blockchain services have suffered. As a point of reference, at one point PayPal was losing $10 MM USD per month due to fraud.

To summarize: there is never a valid reason to store bitcoin off the blockchain.

How Are My Private Keys Accessed?

Your bitcoin private keys are kept on encrypted USB drives in our US bank vaults. In order to sign a transaction with your private key, two members of our transaction signing committee must simultaneously enter their decryption keys into an offline machine inside the bank vault. In other words, no single individual has access to your private funds.

Because CoinSafe is on the blockchain all customers always know that 100% of their funds have never left their account. By using the very same cryptography that secures the Bitcoin protocol, it's like having a vault with an unfakeable one-way camera for all our users to see.

What Happens if I Designate a Guardian and They Block My Withdrawals?

Remember that the primary purpose of the Guardian is to protect your account, so you want to choose someone that you trust. However, relationships can sour and we have to plan for the worst. Fundamentally, the account belongs to you, so if we can authenticate that you really do want to withdraw the funds then we can remove your Guardian's status.

Authenticating you is harder than it sounds (there are lots of people who would like to impersonate you!) so we go to great lengths to be sure that it's really you trying to remove your Guardian. We can use lots of additional signals to authenticate that you're you, depending on the specifics of your case:

  • Knowledge Based Authentication - We give you a series of questions (e.g. which of the following streets have you lived near in the last 5 years?) that you must answer instantly. We can do this easily because we verified all your info when you created your account.
  • Login History - We log all activity on the site, so we can tell if you're logging in from the same browsers and IP addresses that you've previously used to access the site.
  • Secondary Documents Scans - Identification documents, utility bills, bank statements, etc
  • Licensed US Notary - Notarized documents from a licensed US notary who has verified your identity document in person to confirm it matches what we have on file.

No one of these signals is sufficient to restore access to an account, and in a very rare case that is highly contested we may require a valid court order in order to override your guardian's consent. Our policy is to always err on the side of caution, meaning that if we can't authenticate an account we will take our time to sort it out. During this process, the funds never move and this is always publicly visible on the bitcoin blockchain.
