For Business

{% trans "What Does CoinSafe Do?" %}

{% blocktrans %} CoinSafe makes it easy for individuals and small businesses to connect offline to buy and sell bitcoin. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Back to Top" %}

{% trans "Does my Cashier/Teller Need to Understand Bitcoin?" %}

{% blocktrans %} Not at all! The app is designed to be used by someone who has no idea what bitcoin is. All the teller needs to do is confirm when they have recieved cash (so that the app can pay out bitcoin). The app will also monitor the bitcoin network and notify the teller when it is safe to pay out cash to a customer (if buying bitcoin). {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Back to Top" %}

{% trans "When I Buy Bitcoin from a Customer, How Long Do I Have to Wait Before Releasing the Cash?" %}

{% blocktrans %} Bitcoin transactions take 10 minutes on average to be included in a block, though it varies greatly. While double-spend attacks are rare, we recommend waiting for one confirmation just to be safe. You can set your own number of confirmations if you like. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans with coindesk_url='http://www.coindesk.com/information/how-do-bitcoin-transactions-work/' %} You can read more about how bitcoin transactions work in this article from CoinDesk {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Can I Really Set Whatever Price I Want?" %}

{% blocktrans %} Yes, but you can't force customers to transact with you at those prices. Just like any other product, you have to charge a competitive price if you want to get business. CoinSafe transparently displays your price markup to customers, so they can see exactly what they're paying. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} That said, providing instant bitcoin in person provides a lot of benefits and so it can command retail pricing. Your customers don't have to have a bank account (or link it to a third party exchange) nor wait several days for their fiat transactions to process. Cash is instantaneous. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Does CoinSafe Have My Bitcoin Private Keys?" %}

{% trans "No." %}

{% blocktrans with coinbase_url='https://coinbase.com/' blockchain_url='https://blockchain.info/' bitstamp_url='https://www.bitstamp.net/' %} If you want to sell bitcoin to customers (for cash), CoinSafe has to have a way to send the funds once the transaction is complete, so we do this via API. The way this works is that you grant us access to your Coinbase, blockchain.info, or Bitstamp wallet. For security, we always recommend keeping low balances on third party bitcoin sites. Remember that you always have absolute power to unlink a third party wallet from CoinSafe by revoking the credential. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} If all you want to do is buy bitcoin from customers (with the cash in your cash register), then all CoinSafe needs is a valid bitcoin address for customers to send your funds to. That can be any address and we won't even know who holds the private key or what software or service you used to generated it. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Can I Accept Credit Cards for Bitcoin Purchases?" %}

{% blocktrans %} You can, but we strongly recommend against it. Besides the transactions fees they charge, there is a very high risk of chargeback. Someone could use a stolen credit card (or use their own card and later claim it was stolen) and then your payment processor might take that money back from you weeks later. Remember that bitcoin transactions are irreversible, so if that happens you would have lost both the money and the bitcoin. Yikes! {% endblocktrans %}

For Customers

{% trans "Am I Getting a Fair Price?" %}

{% blocktrans with lb_url='https://localbitcoins.com/' %} We display prices very transparently so you know exactly what price you're getting and what percentage markup it is from the major exchanges. Plus, you can easily shop around between different businesses on CoinSafe to find the ones with the best price. Bitcoin in person is always more expensive than online exchanges because you're getting bitcoin immediately (no multiple business day waiting periods) and without having to have a bank account (or link it to a third party). A good baseline for comparing prices is localbitcoins.com. {% endblocktrans %}

After a few weeks, if the bitcoin is unclaimed they will return it to the business you transacted with. Don't forget to claim your bitcoin! {% endblocktrans %}
